I am a software engineer with over four years of experience working on systems where reliability, correctness, and long-term maintainability matter. My background spans automotive and medical domains, where software is expected to behave predictably under real-world constraints.

I primarily work with C++ and Qt, and I am comfortable operating across different layers of the stack — from application logic and user interfaces to debugging system-level issues on Linux-based environments.

How I approach engineering

I value clarity over complexity and prefer solutions that are easy to reason about, test, and maintain. I pay close attention to edge cases, failure modes, and long-term impact rather than short-term fixes.

Working in safety-critical and regulated environments has shaped my approach to development: careful analysis, structured debugging, and respect for process where it adds real value.

Current focus

Alongside professional work, I actively use personal projects and open-source contributions to deepen my understanding of system-level programming, tooling, and software design principles.

I am particularly interested in roles that involve complex systems, long-lived codebases, and collaboration with experienced engineering teams.
